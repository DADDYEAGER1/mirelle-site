## Nail Preparation: Foundation for Magnetic Success

Surface preparation determines particle adhesion quality. Buff natural nail gently using fine-grit buffer—180 to 240 grit appropriate. Goal: create slight texture without damaging nail plate. Smooth nails prevent gel adhesion. Over-buffed nails show ridges through finished manicure. Light buffing achieves optimal adhesion surface.

Dehydrator application removes natural oils preventing gel lifting. Apply thin layer across entire nail plate including free edge. Allow air drying thirty seconds before proceeding. Cure sixty seconds under LED lamp. Base coat should appear completely dry and slightly tacky on surface. This tackiness allows magnetic gel polish to grip properly.

Optional base color application enhances magnetic effect. Black gel base creates maximum contrast making pink magnetic shimmer intensely visible. ## Mastering Magnet Technique: Distance, Timing, Angles

<strong>Hover distance</strong> critically affects particle response. Position magnet two to three millimeters above wet polish. Closer produces intense concentrated line. Further creates softer, broader shimmer band. Consistent distance across all nails ensures matching appearance. Use visual reference—magnet should nearly touch polish without actual contact.

Hold time determines particle migration completion. Ten to fifteen seconds allows sufficient particle movement creating defined magnetic line. Magnet angle controls pattern direction. Vertical positioning creates straight line down nail center—classic cat eye. Diagonal angle produces slanted shimmer effect. Horizontal orientation generates curved line following magnet edge. Experiment with angles creating varied Valentine designs. Maintain chosen angle consistently across matching nails ensuring symmetry between hands.

Movement patterns introduce complexity. Stationary magnet produces single focused line. Slow magnet movement during hold time creates gradient shimmer—particles follow magnet path. Advanced technique requires practice—uneven movement creates irregular particle distribution. Begin with stationary positioning mastering basic technique before attempting movement variations.

Flash cure sets particle position. After magnetic manipulation, cure nail fifteen to twenty seconds only. This partial cure freezes particle placement preventing settling while maintaining gel flexibility. Full cure at this stage would prevent second coat application if coverage appears uneven. ## Troubleshooting Common Cat Eye Challenges

<strong>Weak magnetic effect</strong> indicates insufficient particle concentration or improper magnet positioning. Solution: verify gel polish labeled as magnetic formula—standard gel won't respond. Check magnet proximity—must hover within three millimeters for strong particle pull. If using older magnetic gel, particles may have settled in bottle. Roll bottle between palms gently redistributing particles before application.

Uneven particle distribution creates patchy shimmer. Cause: inconsistent gel coat thickness or premature settling. Solution: ensure even polish application across entire nail surface. Work quickly transitioning from application to magnetic manipulation within five seconds. If uneven appearance occurs, wipe nail clean with alcohol and restart application immediately before gel begins curing.

<strong>Particles settling prematurely</strong> happens when working too slowly. Multiple nails attempted simultaneously causes first applications to settle before magnet contact. Solution: complete entire process—application, magnetic manipulation, flash cure—on single nail before proceeding to next. This sequential approach prevents settling while maintaining consistency.

Magnetic line lacks definition appearing blurry or spread. Issue: magnet held too far from nail surface or moved during hold time. Solution: decrease hover distance to two millimeters. Maintain absolutely steady hand position during ten to fifteen second hold. Brace hand against stable surface if tremor concerns exist. Clean, defined line requires motionless magnet positioning.

Gel cured before magnetic manipulation completed. Rushed lamp proximity or accidental finger placement under lamp edge causes premature curing. Solution: position lamp away from work area during application and magnetic phases. Only cure deliberately after magnet removed and desired effect achieved. If accidental cure occurs, file surface gently, apply fresh gel layer, repeat magnetic process.

## Sealing and Longevity: Top Coat Application

<strong>Top coat selection</strong> affects magnetic shimmer preservation. No-wipe gel formulas cure to glossy finish without sticky residue requiring removal. Standard gel top coats work equally well—wipe residue with alcohol after curing. Avoid thick viscosity top coats that can drag through magnetic particles disturbing alignment. Thin, self-leveling formulas preserve particle positioning.

Application technique prevents particle disruption. Pour small top coat amount onto palette rather than working directly from bottle. This prevents magnetic particle contamination of entire top coat supply. Use light brush pressure floating top coat over magnetic surface rather than pressing firmly. Heavy pressure can shift particles creating blurred shimmer line.

Cap free edges deliberately during top coat phase. Pull brush under nail tip edge creating seal preventing moisture penetration. This encapsulation extends wear time from ten days to two to three weeks. Edge sealing particularly critical for magnetic nails—lifted edges expose gel allowing water entry causing premature separation.

Final cure requires full sixty seconds under LED lamp. Partial cure leaves top coat soft causing premature wear and dulling. Complete polymerization hardens protective layer preserving magnetic effect long-term. Allow nails to cool thirty seconds after cure before touching—immediate pressure on warm gel can create fingerprint impressions.

Maintenance extends magnetic cat eye longevity. Apply cuticle oil daily maintaining gel flexibility and preventing cracking. Wear gloves during household cleaning—harsh chemicals degrade gel top coat. Avoid prolonged water submersion weakening adhesion at nail edges. Refresh top coat every five to seven days adding protective layer and restoring dimensional shine.
