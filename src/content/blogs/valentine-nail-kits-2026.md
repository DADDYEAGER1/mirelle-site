What matters: <strong>UV lamp</strong> wattage (48W minimum or cure times triple), <strong>gel polish</strong> viscosity (thick formulas streak for beginners), and color authenticity. Valentine palettes photograph beautifully but often arrive sheer, requiring four coats for opacity.

## Getting More Than Three Days of Wear

<strong>Gel longevity</strong> depends more on prep work than formula quality. Polish the <strong>nail surface</strong> until matte. Any shine indicates oils causing lifting. File front edges and sides, then dehydrate with rubbing alcohol or proper <strong>nail prep</strong> solution. This step determines whether manicure lasts three days or two weeks.

<strong>Base coat application</strong> technique matters significantly. Thin layers cure better than thick ones. Cap the free edge (brush gel over tip) to seal and prevent water penetration. Most early chipping starts at tips because users skip this step, according to <a href="https://www.sallybeauty.com/nails/nails-by-type/gel-nails/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">professional gel nail supplies</a> guidance.

<strong>Curing time</strong> directly affects durability. Kit instructions say 60 seconds? Use 90. Undercured gel feels slightly tacky even after curing and peels within days. If you're experiencing consistent early failure, you're likely undercuring. Add 30 seconds to recommended times, especially with budget lamps.

<strong>Top coat</strong> quality creates protective barrier preventing chips. Cheap top coats feel thin and don't level properly. If your kit's included top coat isn't performing (you'll know by day three), invest in separate professional-grade top coat. It's $12 upgrade doubling wear time across any kit.

Daily wear impacts longevity significantly. Typing, washing dishes, physical activities stress gel nails. Users with desk jobs report 10-14 day wear; healthcare workers average 5-7 days. That's mechanical reality, not formula failure.

## When Good Kits Go Wrong

Thick, goopy formula indicates old stock or improper storage. <strong>Gel polish</strong> thickens over time, especially with temperature fluctuations. If your new kit arrives with syrupy consistency, it was warehouse-stored improperly. Most sellers replace defective units.

Lamp failure patterns reveal quality immediately. Units dying after one use suggest defective components; failure after weeks indicates cheap parts wearing out. Amazon's return window covers early failures, but lamps dying at day 35 leave you without recourse.

Peeling within 48 hours points to prep failures, not formula issues. Users blame the kit when actual problem is skipping dehydration or over-filing nails. If you're getting consistent early peeling across proper prep steps, then formula adhesion is genuinely weak.

Color mismatches between product photos and actual bottles plague <strong>Valentine kits</strong> especially. That vibrant coral-red photographs beautifully but arrives as sheer orange-pink requiring four coats. Read recent reviews specifically mentioning color accuracy before buying.

Bottles arriving broken or leaking suggest poor packaging. <strong>Gel polish</strong> shipping in extreme temperatures can compromise seals. Inspect packages immediately and document damage for return claims.

Most kit problems resolve with technique adjustments rather than product replacement. Invest time learning proper application through tutorials before declaring kit defective.
